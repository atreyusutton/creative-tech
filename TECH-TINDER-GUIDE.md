# 👉👈 Technology Tinder Assignment Guide

## What This Assignment Is Really About

This is the opening move of ATLS 5410. The instructor is not testing your tech knowledge — they're learning:
- How you think about technology
- What you value
- What kind of critique you're comfortable making
- Why you're in this program

**"Technology Tinder" is just a framing device** to force you to:
- Take a position
- Justify it personally
- Accept that others may disagree

**Disagreement = success here.**

## Core Question You Must Answer

**Which technologies do you personally believe make humans more capable — and which make us less so?**

NOT:
- ❌ What's trendy
- ❌ What's objectively "good"
- ❌ What sounds academic

BUT:
- ✅ What you actually care about and believe

## What You Must Present (Hard Requirements)

### 1. Two Technologies Total

**Swipe Right 👉** → A technology you believe adds real human value

**Swipe Left 👈** → A technology you believe reduces human capability or intention

These can be:
- Totally different technologies
- **OR** two applications of the same technology (this is often stronger)

### 2. Presentation Format

- **3 minutes total** (short — practice this)
- **At least 2 slides:**
  - 1 slide = Swipe Right
  - 1 slide = Swipe Left
- Slides can be:
  - Minimal
  - Visual
  - Text-light

**(This is NOT a design class yet)**

### 3. Content Expectations (This Matters)

For each technology, you must explain:
- **What it is**
- **Why you personally value or reject it**
- **What it enables or disables in people**
- **Why that matters to you**

⚠️ **You are expected to be subjective.**

If someone can't tell who you are from this presentation, you didn't go far enough.

## What NOT to Do

❌ Don't try to be neutral  
❌ Don't pick something you don't care about  
❌ Don't hedge every statement with "on the other hand…"  
❌ Don't optimize for being liked

**The instructor literally says:**
> "If people are perturbed, you may have succeeded."

## Example Approaches (to spark thinking)

### Strong Approach 1: Same Tech, Different Applications

**Swipe Right:** AI for protein folding (AlphaFold)
- Why: Amplifies human scientific capability, accelerates research that saves lives
- Enables: Scientists to do in days what took years
- Matters because: Augments human intelligence without replacing human judgment

**Swipe Left:** AI content generation (generic blog posts)
- Why: Diminishes human creativity, floods internet with meaningless content
- Disables: Critical thinking, original expression, authentic voice
- Matters because: Optimizes for quantity over quality, devalues human craft

### Strong Approach 2: Different Technologies, Clear Values

**Swipe Right:** Open-source hardware (Arduino, Raspberry Pi)
- Why: Democratizes technology creation, puts tools in anyone's hands
- Enables: Learning by making, community knowledge sharing, accessibility
- Matters because: I believe technology should empower, not gatekeep

**Swipe Left:** Algorithmic engagement optimization (infinite scroll, autoplay)
- Why: Hijacks human attention, optimizes for addiction not value
- Disables: Intentional choice, deep focus, agency over time
- Matters because: I believe technology should serve human goals, not manipulate them

### Strong Approach 3: Take a Controversial Stand

**Swipe Right:** Analog tools (film cameras, vinyl, mechanical keyboards)
- Why: Forces intentionality, creates meaningful friction, values craft
- Enables: Deeper engagement, mastery, appreciation of process
- Matters because: Not all efficiency is good; some slowness makes us more human

**Swipe Left:** Frictionless UX design (one-click purchasing, auto-fill everything)
- Why: Removes opportunity for reflection and intentional choice
- Disables: Mindfulness, consideration, conscious decision-making
- Matters because: Ease isn't always what we need; sometimes resistance builds character

## Planning Your Presentation

### Step 1: Pick Technologies You Actually Care About

Ask yourself:
- What technology genuinely excites me? Why?
- What technology frustrates or concerns me? Why?
- What do these feelings reveal about my values?

### Step 2: Clarify Your Position

For each technology, write:
- **One sentence:** What it is
- **One sentence:** My position on it
- **One sentence:** What it enables/disables
- **One sentence:** Why that matters to me personally

### Step 3: Design Minimal Slides

Each slide should have:
- **Technology name/image**
- **Your position** (1-2 sentences max)
- **Key point** (what it enables/disables)

That's it. You'll speak the rest.

### Step 4: Practice for Time

3 minutes is SHORT. Practice:
- Intro (15 sec): "I swiped right on X and left on Y"
- Swipe Right (1 min): Explain what, why, what it enables, why it matters
- Swipe Left (1 min): Same structure
- Close (30 sec): What this reveals about what you want to build

## Tips for a Strong Presentation

### Do:
✅ Speak from personal experience  
✅ Use concrete examples  
✅ Take a clear, strong position  
✅ Show your values  
✅ Welcome disagreement

### Don't:
❌ Apologize for your opinion  
❌ List features without position  
❌ Try to please everyone  
❌ Hide behind academic language  
❌ Make it about the tech alone (make it about humans)
